Green Menu

Green Menu represents a digital solution to a very important environmental issue. At first glance it looks like a normal menu that is displayed on a tablet inside a restaurant. However, it has a green side. Too good to waste and Local Goods. Too good to waste contains two pages: Delicious Mistakes and Too good to bin them. Delicious Mistakes contains dishes that either lack the correct ingredients or aren’t aesthetically pleasing. Too good to bin them contains dishes that have ingredients that will go to waste by the end of that shift due to their expiration date. Local Goods contains exclusive dishes that have at least one locally sourced ingredient. These dishes have a discounted price too due to their transportation cost being reduced. These dishes are exclusive because their time on the menu is limited to local stock. The menu also displays a layer mask with “Currently unavailable” which reduces the risk of a client ordering something that is not available. The menu is updated in real time by one of the chefs.
